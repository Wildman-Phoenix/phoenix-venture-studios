# RSS Cinematic Cover Standards

Use these standards when creating or reviewing Phoenix Founder Signal cover images.

## Cover Grammar

- Start with the article's strongest context, not the source image.
- Translate that context into one cinematic metaphor: keyhole, threshold, ring, silhouette, device layer, workflow stack, trust boundary, valuation room, or pressure field.
- Build the image like an editorial magazine cover, not a product render or stock illustration.
- Keep one dominant visual idea. Supporting elements should clarify the metaphor, not compete with it.
- Leave intentional negative space for a bold headline overlay.

## What Works

- A keyhole or doorway that turns privacy/context into a physical threshold.
- A human silhouette facing a system, ring, gateway, or decision point.
- App/context cards arranged as a memory layer, not as random floating UI.
- Layered device/system diagrams when the article is about architecture or compute.
- Cinematic light, shadow, reflections, and scale.
- Minimal color systems: black, cream, titanium, warm gold, electric blue, and one Phoenix-orange accent.

## What Fails

- Generic boardrooms, stock offices, showroom desks, and literal product spreads.
- Too many devices or duplicate devices.
- Decorative AI clouds that do not explain the story.
- Overstuffed UI panels, unreadable fake dashboards, and random app windows.
- Exact brand logos or trademark marks unless rights are explicitly approved.
- Images that look like a screenshot, ad mockup, or slide instead of a cover.

## RSS Prompt Rule

For each article, extract:

1. The hidden strategic tension.
2. The physical or symbolic boundary.
3. The operator/founder implication.
4. The one visual metaphor that makes the above obvious at thumbnail size.

Then generate the cover from that metaphor.
